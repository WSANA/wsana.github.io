Massachusetts Medical Marijuana Initiative

In 2012 a ballot initiative was passed that allows cannabis to be used for medical purposes in the state, eliminating state criminal and civil penalties related to the medical. Since then Massachusetts Department of Public Health has been responsible for the application process to place dispensaries across the state.

There have been 22 applications submitted by organizations seeking to open locations. Four of these applications have been in Boston.

Green Heart Holistic Health has proposed to open a 3,000 square foot dispenary in the lot formerly occupied by Skiptons Pet Store on the corner of Massachussets Ave and South Hampton Street.

The residents and business of the South End are opposed to this applicant and the placement of dispensary.

Neigbhorhood Position Statement The following is the WSANA position. These position statements have been made public in our neigbhorhood meetings and shared with our elected officials. We welcome your input.

Volnerable Population

The South End services a range of volunerable services that we believe will be negativly impacted by the placement of a dispency in the neigbhorhood.

The neighborhood and business support the services and people who visit the area. We value our role in the city and want to see the city provides the support to make all parties safe and secure.

Flawed Application

The MASS DPH licensing and application process is flawed. The application submitted by the Green Heart Holistic Health contains a substantial number of inaccuracies and misrepresentations.

MASS DPH have provided little visibility in the selection process, application process and status of concerns. Lack of transparency has degraded the publics confidence in the states managment of this law.

Drug Sales

There is an longstanding illegal prescription drug problem associated with the Boston Medical Center’s on-site pharmacy. The prescriptions legally dispensed by BMC flow out in the surrounding neighborhood and are resold within sight of the pharmacy. The available supply of prescription drugs for sale make the area a destination for people engaged in illegal activities.

 

Lack of Response

The City of Boston and the BMC administrators have not been able or willing to address the criminal behavior either on the hospital grounds or in the immediate area around the hospital.

There has been a 25% increase in automobile brake-ins and street crime in the past six months.

Increase in Crime

The placement of the Medical Marijuana dispensary will increase crime in the area surrounding the Boston Medical Center and neighborhood.

There has been a 25% increase in automobile brake-ins and street crime in the past six months. Neither the City or the hosptal have addressed neighborhood concerns.

BMC Pharmacy Problems

The City of Boston’s failure to address the neighborhood concerns with the BMC pharmacy do not lead WSANA to believe that any issue with the dispensary will be addressed.

Statement of Oppostion

WSANA and its members oppose the application process and the placement of a Medical Marijuana Dispensary at its currently proposed or in any location within reach of the South End. We have a shared belief that the placement of this dispensary will act a tipping point that will dramatically and negatively impact our quality of life, public safety and the services of those who visit Boston Medical center to receive important health care services.
