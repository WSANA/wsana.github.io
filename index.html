Mass. Medical Marijuana Initiative

In 2012 a ballot initiative was passed that allows cannabis to be used for medical purposes in the state, eliminating state criminal and civil penalties related to the medical. Since then Massachusetts Department of Public Health has been responsible for the application process to place dispensaries across the state.

There have been 22 applications submitted by organizations seeking to open locations. Four of these applications have been in Boston.

The Residents and Businesses of the South End Neighborhood are opposed to this applicant, the application process and the placement of a Medical Marijuana Dispensary in any location within reach of the South End. We have a shared belief that the placement of this dispensary will act a tipping point that will dramatically and negatively impact our quality of life, public safety and the services of those who visit Boston Medical center to receive important health care services.

Neigbhorhood Opposition The members of the Worcester Square Area Neighborhood Association, including many businesses in the area, are overwhelmingly opposed to the placement of a marijuana dispensary in the area of Boston Medical Center, including the New Market area. The overloading of social services, some dealing with substance abuse issues -- methadone clinics, the Woods-Mullen Shelter, which offers shelter to anyone, including those who are intoxicated, Boston Medical Center`s drug dispensaries, which are filling legal prescriptions that are sometimes sold or traded outside the hospital -- is causing a flood of unintended consequences for residents of the South End. On any given day, used needles and empty liquor bottles can be found in yards along Massachusetts Avenue. People are often seen staggering down the street, sometimes collapsing and sleeping on sidewalks. They can also be seen lounging on the steps of homes, eating meals and leaving all sorts of refuse behind. We believe the adding of a marijuana dispensary will act as a tipping point, which will dramatically impact the safety and quality of life of the surrounding community.

Flawed Application Process

The MASS DPH licensing and application process is flawed. The application submitted by the Green Heart Holistic Health contains a substantial number of inaccuracies and misrepresentations.

MASS DPH have provided little visibility in the selection process, application process and status of concerns. Lack of transparency has degraded the publics confidence in the states managment of this law.

MDPH sent applications to an out of state firm to examine and score. That firm had no way of knowing the veracity expressed by the applicants.

Social & Medical Services The South End and Boston Medical Center provide a variety of social and medical services to a large at-risk population as well as the South End (and Boston area) population at-large.

Woods-Mullen Shelter @ 784 Mass Ave.

The Woods Mullen Shelter is an emergency shelter located at the corner of Melnea Cass Boulevard and Massachusetts Avenue, near the Boston Medical Center. Woods Mullen Shelter provides food, emergency clothing, health care and case management services to anyone in need, including those who are intoxicated.
Homeless Shelter Wet Shelter

Weintrab Clinic @ 794 Mass Ave.

Homeless shelter with 190 beds, open 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Hosts a day clinic that provides counceling and mental health services.
Homeless Shelter Treatment

Boston Medical Center @ 111 Mass Ave.

City Hospital, with 400 doctors and nursed, servicing 1500 patients each day, with trauma inpatient and outpatient services and several drug dispensaries at which prescriptions are filled and then sold or traded outside on Massachusetts Avenue.
Out-patient Dispensary Emergency Room

Pine Street Inn @ 200 Berkly Ave.

Homeless shelter that provides permanent supportive housing, job training and placement, emergency shelter and street outreach to more than 1,600 homeless men and women each day.
Dry Shelter Training

Long Island Shelter @ Long Island, MA

The Long Island Shelter runs a daily bus service that brings 50 to 100 shelter guests to the South End and the Boston Medical Center each day for access to medical and social services.
Homeless Shelter Daily Drop-Off
