MA Medical Marijuana Initiative

In 2012, Massachusetts voters approved a ballot initiative to allow cannabis to be used for medical purposes, eliminating state criminal and civil penalties related to such use. The ballot initiative designated the Massachusetts Department of Public Health (MA DPH) with the responsibility for the application process to place dispensaries across the state.

Twenty-two (22) applications were submitted by organizations seeking to open dispensaries, four of which were for locations in Boston. MA DPH has given provisional approval to two (2) of these Boston applications. One of these is from Green Heart Holistic Health, which is proposing to locate a dispensary at 70 Southampton Street (the former location of Skipton’s Kennels) in the Newmarket industrial district, at the edge of the South End.

Many Residents and Businesses of the South End Neighborhood are opposed both to the application process conducted by the MA DPH and the placement of a Medical Marijuana Dispensary in any location in or near the South End. We have a shared belief that the placement of this dispensary will dramatically and negatively impact our quality of life, public safety and those who visit Boston Medical Center and other facilities to receive important services.

Neighborhood Opposition The Worcester Square Area Neighborhood Association (WSANA), residents, and many businesses in the area are overwhelmingly opposed to the placement of a marijuana dispensary at 70 Southampton Street and in the general area of the South End.

The neighborhood is already overloaded with social services. It is home to agencies that provide assistance for medical services, substance abuse, counseling, half-way houses and homeless shelters. Boston Medical Center dispenses legal prescriptions that are frequently sold or traded outside the hospital.

The overloading of these services in one area is causing a flood of unintended consequences for South End residents. On any given day, one can see open-air drug dealing. Used needles and empty liquor bottles are found in yards in the neighborhood. People are often seen staggering down the street, sometimes collapsing and sleeping on sidewalks. We believe adding a marijuana dispensary will act as a tipping point, further impacting the safety and quality of life of the community.

Flawed Application Process

The MA DPH application review and approval process is flawed. The application submitted by the Green Heart Holistic Health contains a serious inaccuracies and misrepresentations.

DPH has provided little visibility in the selection and application process. It appears tone deaf to the community's concerns. The lack of transparency has degraded the publics’s confidence in DPH's management of this law.

Quality of Life Concerns

There is a long-standing drug dealing problem associated with the prescription of drugs legally dispensed from the Boston Medical Center’s on-site pharmacies. Drugs issued from the BMC pharmacies are resold, making the surrounding neighborhood an open-air drug market. This activity is regularly witnessed by neighborhood residents. Additionally, the back alleys of the the neighborhood and many other parts of the South End are locations where crack cocaine, marijuana and heroin are used, even in broad daylight. Used hypodermic needles are found in front yards and alleys with alarming frequency.
